# Phase G G1 Contract Freeze — Messaging and Claims Governance

**Date:** 2026-04-01  
**Phase:** Phase G (`phaseg`)  
**Status:** Frozen

---

## 1) Approved Claims Boundary (locked)

External messaging is limited to evidence-bound claims only.

Allowed claim category:
- statements that are directly supported by verified internal artifacts and closure outputs

Forbidden claim category:
- any unverified capability claim
- claims implying roadmap completion beyond verified phase status
- unsupported benchmark, performance, reliability, or security superiority claims
- implied guarantees not explicitly verified and documented

---

## 2) Messaging Guardrails (what can/cannot be said)

### Can say
- verified phase completion states exactly as recorded in closure packets
- capability statements backed by current evidence artifacts
- bounded roadmap status with explicit blocked/in-progress/closed distinctions

### Cannot say
- "fully autonomous" or equivalent broad guarantees without explicit verified evidence
- comparative claims against external systems without reproducible evidence
- forward-looking commitments represented as completed outcomes
- claims that collapse planning/authorization/execution/closure into a single state

---

## 3) Traceability Requirement (locked)

Every external claim must map to verifiable internal evidence.

Minimum mapping rule:
- each claim references at least one current authoritative artifact path
- claim-to-evidence mapping must be preserved in campaign review records

Publication is blocked when claim traceability is missing or ambiguous.

---

## 4) Channel-Safe Messaging Shape (locked)

Across approved channels, messaging must remain:

- concise
- evidence-referenced
- non-speculative
- status-accurate

Recommended claim form:
1. short claim
2. phase/status qualifier
3. evidence pointer

---

## 5) Review and Escalation Rules (locked)

Required pre-publication reviews:

- factual consistency review
- governance/claims-boundary review

Escalation triggers:

- uncertainty about evidence mapping
- claim language that implies unverified guarantees
- conflicts between message text and closure packet status

Escalated items require explicit disposition record before publication.

---

## 6) Non-goals / Forbidden Messaging Shortcuts

Explicitly forbidden:

- hype-first wording that outruns verified evidence
- unsupported benchmark language
- implied enterprise/compliance guarantees without evidence
- omission of known blocked states where context requires them
- channel-specific wording that weakens claims-boundary enforcement

Any exception requires explicit audit approval.
